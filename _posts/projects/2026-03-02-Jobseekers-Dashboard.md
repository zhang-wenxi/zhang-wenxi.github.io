What this visual tells us immediately is that our fairness is not a guessing game—it is a structure. Look at the Professional and Middle Management segments. Notice the sheer density and thickness of the ribbons flowing out of them. This shows that we are successfully concentrating our 'salary lifeblood' exactly where leadership and expertise reside. We aren't just paying for years of service; we are paying for the core engines of our business.
However, the real story of fairness is in the symmetry. Follow the paths for the Project Manager and the Senior Manager. You see ribbons of significant, healthy thickness bridging these roles to our upper management tiers. This is a visual promise to every employee that there is a clear, high-value 'flow' from where they are now to where they can go. It proves that our 'Executive' and 'Professional' labels aren't just empty names—they are backed by a proportional commitment of resources.
But the most important conclusion is this: Look at how the Junior Executive and Fresh Entry levels connect to specialized roles like Quantity Surveyor or Project Engineer. The ribbons are strong and distinct. This tells our talent that we don't make them 'wait' for fairness. We recognize market value the moment they step through the door.
My conclusion? Our salary flow is balanced. We aren't leaking value into the wrong places. We have built a system where the weight of the role matches the width of the reward. We are paying fairly because we are paying for impact."
